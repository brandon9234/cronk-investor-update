A short friends-and-family update on what we are building and where Cronk is headed next.

Hi everyone,

I know a lot of you have been wondering what is happening with Cronk, so I thought I would start sending a short update every other week on what we are building, where things stand, and how you can help if something comes to mind.

If you know anyone who might be interested in our software, our fulfillment network, or joining the company in some capacity, please send them my way. We are still early, but things are moving quickly.

Right now, our biggest focus is getting our warehouse network fully operational. We are setting up both the Jacobus, Pennsylvania warehouse and the Tyler, Texas warehouse. Alongside our Meridian, Idaho operations, this three-warehouse network will allow us to reach:

98.1% of the U.S. with two-day shipping
24.9% of the U.S. with next-day shipping

That is a major advantage compared to most competitors in our category, who typically offer 3 to 5 business day shipping. We have already seen with our own listings that faster delivery is especially powerful in the gifting space, where timing often determines whether a customer buys or keeps looking.

This warehouse network also helps us save money on shipping costs by placing production closer to customers.

Warehouse network

Both the Texas and Pennsylvania warehouses will be operational with UV printers and CO2 lasers by the end of next week. In the meantime, the Boise/Meridian warehouse is fulfilling all current demand with expedited shipping times.

On the software side, Cronk allows orders to be ingested from all three platforms and automatically generate production-ready images based on SKU. As orders come in, the system knows what product was purchased, generates the correct customized artwork, and routes it toward fulfillment.

Software demos

Foundry and AI video generation are just two of the features we have been building. Cronk Foundry makes it easy to create AI and design previews for products that can be pushed into Shopify, and it will support our own products while also being sold to commercial clients.

We have also finished automated inventory tracking, warehouse splitting, and nesting. Label generation is in the works, along with a listing generator that will let users create listings inside Cronk and automatically populate them to Amazon, Etsy, and Shopify while formatting each listing for the specific e-commerce platform.

That listing workflow will also support AI-generated product thumbnails and videos. We have already seen on our own products that these assets can be made faster, cheaper, and at higher quality than traditional designer-made thumbnails.

The goal is simple: combine personalized product creation, automated order processing, and faster fulfillment into one system that gives us a meaningful edge in every custom category, not only on price but also on speed.

These categories can seem small individually, but they are extremely large. We saw this at Mayberry, where the custom street sign industry alone on Etsy is multiple millions of dollars a year. The nameplates market is much larger, and the customization industry as a whole is billions and billions of dollars a year. For context, MarketResearch.com / Global Industry Analysts estimates the global personalized gifts market at $37.8 billion in 2024, growing to $52.8 billion by 2030 at a 5.7% CAGR, while Arizton estimates the U.S. personalized gifts market at $9.69 billion in 2024, growing to $14.56 billion by 2030 at a 7.03% CAGR.

We really believe Cronk has a unique ability to move quickly in this market. Our young team, strong engineering talent, and management team are built to create features and launch products fast, while adapting to the new paradigm of AI-assisted coding and AI image generation inside the customization space.

We think this shift will be extremely lucrative for the companies that adopt it fastest. Cronk combines software, regional fulfillment, UI-based previewing, background design generation, and order-to-production automation in a way we have not seen from the current leaders. Larger incumbents may have brand awareness and scale, but they also tend to move slowly, and many are tied to centralized warehouse strategies that are difficult to unwind without major operational disruption.

This AI paradigm shift represents the largest possibility for wealth creation in our lifetimes.
